Say Yes...or No to the Dress

My goal for the project showcases the effect of gender on dress code in 481 schools throughout the United States. Using data gathered by The Pudding, this visualization will showcase the amount schools are being counted and how many target women marketed clothing for dress code violations. The group cluster layout and scrollytelling is a great way to document the number of schools that target clothing by gender.

I hope you have fun scrolling and learn a little something!!!

Schools, schools, schools!!!

481 schools have dress code policies that are directed towards different items of clothing and body parts. Through our findings, these schools punish students based on the item of clothing because they are viewed negatively or unsafe.

Through this story, we showcase ways that schools uphold sexist rules and policies by penalizing women by their clothes items.

Targeted Clothing Items

We take these 481 schools and showcase how many schools target different items of clothing. In this graphic, each bubble represents an item of clothing; the size of the bubbles are dependent on the number of schools that target the items; and the colors are categorized by the market:female,male, or both.

Gender Markets

We take the bubbles representing each item of clothing and separate them towards their marketed gender: female,male, or both.

Through this graph and the size of the bubbles, we see that a lot of schools seem to prohibit clothing that is marketed towards women, which showcases the sexism in dress code policies.
